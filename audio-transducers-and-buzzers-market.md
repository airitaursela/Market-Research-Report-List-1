<p><p>Audio transducers and buzzers are devices that convert electrical signals into sound. The electromagnetic type operates using a coil and magnetic field, producing sound through mechanical movement, commonly found in traditional buzzers. Piezoelectric transducers use materials that generate sound through mechanical deformation when an electric voltage is applied, enabling compact designs with high efficiency. Both types cater to various applications in alarms, indicators, and consumer electronics, with the choice between them often depending on size, sound quality, and power requirements.</p></p>
