<p><p>The nuclear energy market comprises three main types: nuclear fission, nuclear fusion, and nuclear decay. Nuclear fission involves splitting heavy atomic nuclei to release energy, commonly used in power plants. Nuclear fusion, the merging of light atomic nuclei, holds potential for cleaner energy but is still in experimental stages. Nuclear decay, the process by which unstable isotopes release energy as they transform into stable forms, contributes to radioisotope production used in medical applications and research, enhancing overall energy diversity.</p></p>
