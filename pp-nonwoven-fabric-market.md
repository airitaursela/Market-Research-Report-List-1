<p><p>The PP nonwoven fabric market consists of various types including spunbonded, staples, meltblown, and composite fabrics. Spunbonded fabrics are produced by continuously extruding and bonding fibers, offering durability for applications like geotextiles and hygiene products. Staple fabrics utilize short fibers for softness and absorption, typically used in medical and apparel items. Meltblown fabrics involve extruding molten polymer fibers, ideal for filtration applications due to their fine structure. Composite fabrics combine different methods, enhancing performance for specific uses in diverse industries.</p></p>
