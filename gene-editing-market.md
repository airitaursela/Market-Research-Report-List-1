<p><p>The gene editing market comprises three primary technologies: Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFNs), Transcription Activator-Like Effector-based Nucleases (TALENs), and the CRISPR-Cas system. ZFNs utilize engineered proteins to target specific DNA sequences for editing, while TALENs employ customizable proteins to achieve precision in gene modification. The CRISPR-Cas system, a groundbreaking technique, leverages RNA-guided endonucleases for efficient and versatile genome editing. Each market type presents unique advantages, driving applications across agriculture, biotechnology, and therapeutic developments.</p></p>
