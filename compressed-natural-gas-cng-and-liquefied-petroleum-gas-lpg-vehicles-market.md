<p><p>Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) and Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) vehicles are increasingly popular alternatives to traditional gasoline and diesel vehicles. CNG is primarily composed of methane and is stored under pressure, while LPG, a mixture of propane and butane, is stored in liquid form in pressurized tanks. Both types of vehicles offer lower emissions, reduced air pollution, and often lower fuel costs, making them appealing choices for eco-conscious consumers and fleet operators.</p><p>The CNG and LPG vehicles market is experiencing robust growth due to increasing environmental regulations, rising fuel prices, and a growing emphasis on sustainable transportation solutions.
