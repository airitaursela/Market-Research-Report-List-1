Bilayer structures consist of two metal layers, typically aluminum bonded to stainless steel, providing excellent corrosion resistance and mechanical strength. In contrast, three-layer structures add an interlayer, enhancing compatibility and performance between the metals. These configurations are utilized in various industries, including aerospace, automotive, and construction, due to their lightweight nature, durability, and ability to withstand harsh environments, making them highly sought after.</p></p>
