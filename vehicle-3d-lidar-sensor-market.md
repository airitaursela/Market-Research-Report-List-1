<p><p>The Vehicle 3D LiDAR sensor market is primarily divided into two types: Mechanical LiDAR and Solid State LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR utilizes rotating components to scan the environment, providing high-resolution 3D mapping but often at a higher cost and bulkier design. In contrast, Solid State LiDAR is more compact, utilizing fixed components and electronic scanning, leading to lower production costs and improved durability. These distinctions cater to various automotive applications, from advanced driver-assistance systems to autonomous vehicles.</p></p>
